## Why Three Scenarios?

Single-point estimates create false confidence. The realistic scenario assumes everything goes as planned, but product launches rarely do. By modeling pessimistic, realistic, and optimistic scenarios, we:

1. **Show the range of possible outcomes** - Leadership can see best/worst case
2. **Make assumptions explicit** - Forces us to think through what could go wrong (or right)
3. **Enable better decision-making** - "Even if we're wrong by 50%, is this still worth it?"

## Additional Strategic Context

### What We're NOT Modeling:

1. **Retention improvements** - Activated users stay 2.5x longer historically. This could add 50-150% to the LTV calculations.

2. **Viral growth** - Activated users invite teammates 3x more often. Each invite â†’ 40% acceptance â†’ 60% activation â†’ $172.80 LTV.

3. **Support cost reduction** - Better onboarding = fewer support tickets. Estimated 15% reduction in onboarding-related support costs.

4. **Strategic unlock** - Can't scale beyond current growth rate if activation stays at 45%. This feature enables future growth investments.

5. **Competitive necessity** - Competitors (Asana, Linear) have strong onboarding. Not shipping this risks churn to competitors.

### Why These Matter:
If we modeled retention and viral effects, the realistic scenario would be closer to **15-20x ROI**, not 9.4x. We're being conservative.
